Impact of Linear Infrastructure in Biodiversity Conservation in Nepal

Linear infrastructures (LI) such as railways, roadways, and transmission lines significantly threaten biodiversity in Nepal. Roads, in particular, cause forest fragmentation, barriers for wildlife, and roadkill. Railways have similar negative impacts, while transmission lines pose a threat to birds due to electrocution. Despite these threats, no comprehensive study has been conducted in Nepal.

To address this, we are planning a study using geospatial tools to assess the impact of LIs on biodiversity. Currently, we are collecting data on biodiversity and infrastructure, though this has been challenging due to limited data availability in Nepal. Once data collection is complete, we will start the analysis. My primary role in this project has been reviewing literature on the impact of infrastructures on biodiversity conservation.
