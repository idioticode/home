Impact of Single Occurrence Fire on Tree Regeneration and Diversity.

Fire ecology is a relatively under-researched topic in Nepal, despite the occurrence of fire events every year.

We conducted a study on fire ecology to examine the impact of single-event forest fires on tree regeneration and biodiversity. Using Google Earth Engine (GEE), we demarcated regions affected by forest fires for the years 2013, 2015, 2018, and 2021. These regions were selected to ensure that each had experienced a fire event only once within the specified time period, with no subsequent fires.

Field data on tree diversity and tree regeneration were then collected from these demarcated areas. Our analysis revealed that single-event forest fires did not significantly impact tree diversity or tree regeneration.
